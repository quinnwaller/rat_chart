War on Rats? 311 Data Shows Brooklyn Rodents Are Winning the Battle

After NYC established Bedford-Stuyvesant and Bushwick as a Rat Mitigation Zone in April 2023, 311 complaints about rats show that the city’s mitigation efforts are no more successful in the zone than in the rest of Brooklyn.

By Quinn Waller
Nov. 17, 2024

More than a year after NYC designated Bed-Stuy and Bushwick as a Rat Mitigation Zone (RMZ), 311 complaints about rats indicate little progress in the war on rats.

In April 2023, NYC established RMZs and implemented “a data-driven, coordinated, multi-agency effort there to address rats and the conditions that cause them,” according to the City Rule establishing the zones.

But 311 call data, which the city uses to track rat activity, shows mitigation efforts are no more successful in the Bed-Stuy/Bushwick zone than in Brooklyn overall, with complaints declining at a similar or lesser rate.

Alexandra Fochios, an American University law student and author of an Administrative Law Review paper on public policy and rat eradication, said that while the zones are a good idea in theory, “the implementation of these programs can be really limited because cities don’t have the resources to give.”
